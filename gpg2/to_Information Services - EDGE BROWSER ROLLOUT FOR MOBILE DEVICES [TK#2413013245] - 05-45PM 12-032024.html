The Information Services (IS) Department will be removing Chrome from company work phones starting 2025. We will deploy Microsoft Edge now in order to allow some time to migrate your data before 01/01/2025.

 

Everyone should sign into Edge using their company email address (see instructions below if you have not done so already). In doing so, it should reduce the number of times you are asked for your credentials to access company resources.

 

Here are some of the benefits of using Edge over Chrome:

  1. Cross-Platform Sync: Edge allows you to sync your bookmarks, passwords, and settings across all your devices, in this case between your office computer and mobile device.
  2. Privacy and Security: Edge offers robust privacy features, including tracking prevention, InPrivate browsing, and built-in Microsoft Defender SmartScreen to protect against phishing and malware.
  3. Integration with Microsoft Services: Edge provides seamless integration with other Office365 apps such as OneDrive, Excel, Word, etc.
  4. Collections: Edge has a unique feature called Collections, which allows you to organize and save web content, such as images, text, and links, in a more structured way.
  5. Reading Mode and Tools: Edge offers an immersive reader mode that simplifies web pages for better readability. It also includes tools like a built-in PDF reader and annotation capabilities.
  6. Performance and Speed: Edge is known for its efficient performance and speed, often using less memory and CPU resources compared to Chrome.
  7. Battery Efficiency: On mobile devices, Edge is optimized to be more battery-efficient, which can help extend your device’s battery life.
  8. Allow SSO (Single Sign On) to access company’s third-party software platforms without additional username and password authentication.

 

We strongly suggest moving your browser settings over to edge on your mobile and desktop environment. You will have to manually move your bookmarks over on your mobile device. On the desktop, it is much easier to transition:

 

Here’s how you can login to Edge and transfer over your Chrome bookmarks and passwords.

  1. Open the Edge browser

 

  1. Click on the icon of a person in the top left corner and click ‘Sign in to sync data’

 

  1. Follow the Microsoft login

 

  1. Click on the three buttons in the top right of the browser

 

  1. Click on ‘Settings’ in the drop down menu and then select ‘Profiles’ from the menu on the left

 

  1. Under ‘Profile settings’ in the middle column, click on ‘Import browser data’

 

  1. Click on ‘Import’ next to the Import data from Google Chrome

 

  1. Click on Import
