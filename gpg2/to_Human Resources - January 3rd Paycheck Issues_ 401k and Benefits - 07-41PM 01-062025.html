As you know we have switched from ADP to UKG, and it did not go as smoothly as we had anticipated.  There were several payroll issues that occurred, and we have worked tirelessly to get them resolved. If you issue was related to wrong taxes or pay amounts, you need to email the Payroll Team at payroll@autochlor.com

 

401k- What happened

 

The Problem - Two issues:

  1. No deduction occurred when it should have:
    1. This occurred because when we switched from RiversEdge to EPIC Go-Retire, all employees were tasked to register with EPIC and confirm their deferral amounts, if the employee didn’t take that step the deferral amount would remain zero in EPICs system.  ADP would have stayed the same amount, and the deductions still occurred and were deposited into their 401k.  When we switched to UKG, the report uploaded from EPCI still had those employees amounts as zero.  Their task is to now register with EPIC Go-Retire and make sure their deferrals are correct. Otherwise, the amount will stay zero. Individual communications were already sent to those employees. Please read and take action to fix.

 

  1. Deduction did occur but was significantly less than normal:
    1. Due to a formatting issue in the report, the percentages were loaded with an extra decimal place.  For example, if the employee was contributing the deferral amount at 6%.  It was uploaded as .006%. 

 

The Fix: The report was corrected and reloaded on Friday and will have the corrected percentages moving forward.  For those employees that had this error, you will receive an individual communication as required by IRS.

 

Benefits – What happened

 

The Problem – Two Issues:

 

All issues were caused by the same problem in the upload of data from ADP to UKG.  Enrolment end/start dates were not uploaded correctly.

 

  1. Deductions was taken out for plans that employee was not enrolled in
    1. Employees affected by this error will be reimbursed the costs in the January 17th paycheck
  2. Deductions were not taken out for plans employee was enrolled in
    1. Employees affected by this error will receive individualized communications and need to reply if they want the missed deductions to be taken from their January 17th paycheck and/or split over two paychecks January 17th and January 31st.

 

The Fix: The errors were corrected, and report was reuploaded over the weekend.  There will be no interruption in service from insurance companies as the file feeds did not actively flow over to the insurance companies.

 

Please let me know if you have further questions or concerns and please respond to your individualized communications to make sure we get your information accurately corrected.

We sincerely apologize for disruptions this occurred.
