Hi Managers!

 

As we approach the beginning of 2025, HR and Payroll will be processing hundreds of merit pay increases for your teams.

 

Here is a refresher of the steps managers should follow for entering merit pay changes for 2025.

 

Step 1:​

  • Go to the employee's profile​ in BambooHR
  • Click "Request a Change".
    • Are you changing just their compensation? Click “Compensation…”
    • Are you changing their compensation AND job title, i.e. SSR NR -> SSR? Click “Promotion…” instead.

 

Step 2:​

  • A pop up window will appear, set the Effective Date. This needs to be at the beginning of a pay period, on the Sunday or Monday. i.e. 12/29/2024 or 1/12/2025 (if you are on the weekly payroll, it can be the Sunday or Monday of any week)
  • Set the new compensation rate:
    • If you are just increasing the hourly rate, skip to Pay Rate and enter the new rate.
    • If you are moving someone from Hourly to Salary, change the Pay Type then the Pay Rate, then set that to “per Pay Period.” and the Overtime Status. Hourly = Non-exempt, Salary = Exempt. (This should be submitted as a Promotion change because you are most likely also changing the job title as well, i.e. SSR -> OSR)
  • Set the Change Reason, this is usually Merit or Promotion around the beginning of the year.
    • If you are entering a Promotion change, enter the Department and Job Title under Job Information

 

 

 

Step 3:

  • Enter the commission if applicable, including % and effective date.
  • Enter any relevant comments for HR or Payroll to review.
  • Hit Submit

Step 4:

  • Work with Gabriela Williams to ensure that we have relevant commission agreements and promotion letters for this change.

 

Once the change is submitted, it needs to be approved by the Regional Manager and Regional VP. Then it will come to the HR inbox, then Payroll. Once everyone has approved the change, HR will process the change in ADP and UKG.

 

Please reach out to me or the HR team if you have any questions or issues with this process.

 

Thank you,
